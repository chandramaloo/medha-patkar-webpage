Tata Nano Plant Singur

Tata Motors started constructing a factory to manufacture their $2,500 car, the Tata Nano at Singur.[17] Although Medha had studied at an Institute run by Tata empire, yet she protested against the setting up of the plant at Singur, West Bengal. She was of the opinion that local will not get sufficient benefits from plant and they will also loose their fertile land for industrial establishment. On December 2, 2006, Medha was arrested by the police at Singur in West Bengal, for her active involvement in the protest against getting the hold of farmland. She was detained, because the police officers thought that, she might provoke people, thus leading to law and order problem. Medha convoy was assaulted allegedly by CPI(M) activists at Kapaseberia in East Midnapore district while on her way to strife-torn Nandigram.[18] At the height of the agitation, Ratan Tata had made a caustic remark questioning the source of funds of the agitators.[19] In October 2008 Tata announced that the factory will not be completed and the production of the Nano will be set up in Sanand, Gujarat. However, some locals later appealed to Tata Motors to set up the plant at Singur accepting that they were misled.[20]
